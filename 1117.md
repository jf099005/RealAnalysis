# Vitali Covering Lemma
## def.
A family of cubes $k$ is said to cover $E$ in the Vitali sense if, $\forall x\in E$ and $\eta>0$, there is a cube in $K$ containing $x$ whose diameter is less than $\eta$.

## Theorem
Assume that $E$ is covered by $K$ in the Vitali sense and $0<|E|_e<\infty$, then, given $\epsilon>0$, there exist a sequence of **disjoint**, **countable** cubes $\{Q_k\}\subseteq K$ s.t.
- 1. $|E-\cup_j Q_j|=0$
- 2. $\sum_j |Q_j| < (1+\epsilon)|E|_e$

Proof:
by choose an open set $G$ s.t. $E\subset G$ and $|G|=(1+\epsilon)|E|_e$

The second relation holds automatically if we choose cubes containing in $G$

To prove the first relation, by the Simple Vitali Convergence Lemma, $\exists 0<\beta<1$, $\underbrace{Q_1,...,Q_{N_1}}_{disjoint}\subseteq K$ s.t.
$\sum_{j=1}^{N_1}|Q_j| > \beta |E|_e$

Then $|E-\cup_{j}=1^{N_1} Q_j|\le |G-\cup_j Q_j|=|G|-\sum_{j=1}^{N_1} |Q_j|$
$\le (1+\epsilon)|E|_e - \beta |E|_e = (1+\epsilon-\beta)|E|_e$

Taking $\epsilon = \beta/2$, and $E_1= E-\cup_{j=1}^{N_1} Q_j$
$|E_1|\le (1-\beta/2) |E|_e$

For $E_1$, samely, find $Q_{N_1+1},...,Q_{N_2}$ (disjoint from $Q_1,...,Q_N$) s.t.
$\sum{j=N_1+1}^{N_2} |Q_j| > \beta|E_1|_e$

Then we can see that $|E-\cup_{j=1}^{N_2}Q_j|_e = |E-\cup_{j=1}^{N_2} Q_j|\le (1-\beta)^2 |E|_e$


iterate this process, take $Q$ to make $|E_k - \sum_{j=1}^{N_k}Q_j|_e\le (1-\beta/2) |E_k|_e$

Therefore, we can construct a sequence $Q_1,...,Q_{N_k}$ to make
$|E-\cup_{j=1}^{N_k}Q_k|_e\le (1-\beta)^k |E|_e$

take $k\to\infty$, we obtain

$|E-\cup_j Q_j|=0$

## Corollary
Given $\epsilon>0$, $\exists Q_1,...,Q_N$ disjoint cubes in $K$ s.t. $|E-\cup_{j=1}^N Q_j| < \epsilon$ and $\sum_{j=1^N} |Q_j|<(1+\epsilon)|E|_e$

#### remark
$|E|_e = |E-\cup_{j=1}^N Q_j|_e + |E\cap \cup_{j=1}^N Q_j|_e<\epsilon + |E\cap \cup_{j=1}^N Q_j|_e$

$\implies |E\cap \cup_{j=1}^N Q_j|_e > |E|_e-\epsilon$

## Def. [Dini numbres]
$f(x_0)$ is finite
- $D_1 f(x_0) = \limsup_{h\to 0^+} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}$
- $D_2 f(x_0) = \liminf_{h\to 0^+} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}$
- $D_3 f(x_0) = \limsup_{h\to 0^-} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}$
- $D_4 f(x_0) = \liminf_{h\to 0^-} \frac{f(x_0+h)-f(x_0)}{h}$

$f$ differentiable at $x_0$ $\iff$ $D_1 f(x_0)=D_2 f(x_0)=D_3 f(x_0)=D_4 f(x_0)$

In general, $D_1f \ge D_2 f, D_3 f\ge D_4 f$

Also, if $f$ is monotone increasing, then all four numbers are nonnegative.

Let $E=\{x\in (a,b) : D_1 f(x) > D_4 f(x) \}$, we want to show $|E|_e=0$
The same argument can be applied to other pairs of Dini numbers

Let $0<s<r$, define
$A=A_{r,s} = \{ x\in (a,b) : D_1 f(x) > r > s > D_4 f(x) \}$

Then $E=\cup_{r>s,r,s\in\mathbb Q} A_{r,s}$

Claim: $|A|_e=0$

Assume the claim is true, then $f'$ exists almost everywhere in $(a,b)$
